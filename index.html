Who Are We

Our Story

JUDES Community Based Organization was founded and registered in 2018 in Homabay County, inspired by St. Jude, the patron of the desperate. It was established to address critical gaps in underserved rural communities, where health, education, and social equity challenges were deeply interconnected as well as realigning with the United Nations SDGs 1, 3 & 10.

Our beginnings were rooted in collaboration between health professionals, disability advocates, and community leaders determined to break systemic barriers such as stigma, lack of accessible services, and cultural taboos. Since then, we have grown into a trusted force for grassroots transformation and advocacy.

Our Mission

To prioritize the needs of the vulnerable and less fortunate by addressing interconnected challenges in education, health, economic empowerment, and social justice. Our mission is to bridge gaps that limit opportunity, eliminate barriers to access, and nurture environments where dignity, respect, and self-sufficiency are at the core of development.

We achieve this by engaging with local communities, designing sustainable programs, and promoting collective action that strengthens resilience and drives lasting social transformation.

Our Vision

A compassionate and inclusive community where everyone has equal access to resources, opportunities, and support systems needed to thrive. We envision a society where poverty, stigma, and inequality no longer hinder progress, and individuals are empowered to live with dignity, purpose, and fulfillment.

By fostering solidarity, innovation, and accountability, we aim to inspire future generations and build a culture that leaves no one behind.

Our Core Values

Compassion

We care deeply about the well-being of our community members, offering support and kindness especially in times of vulnerability.

Empowerment

We believe in the potential of individuals to create positive change. Through skills, knowledge, and resources, we enable people to take charge of their own development.

Inclusivity

We celebrate diversity and ensure equal opportunities for all, regardless of gender, ability, or background. Inclusivity drives the strength and unity of our community.

Integrity

We operate with transparency, accountability, and ethics in every program and decision, building trust within the communities we serve.

Community

We believe in collaboration, partnerships, and collective action. By working together, we achieve greater impact and sustainable solutions.

Our Programs

We focus on four core areas that empower, protect, and uplift our communities while addressing the most pressing social challenges.

End Sexual & Gender-Based Violence (SGBV)

Fighting SGBV, Restoring Dignity. At Judes CBO, we stand with young women and youth in the fight against sexual and gender-based violence. We strengthen referral pathways to ensure survivors are supported — not silenced. Through access to health services, legal aid, psychosocial support, safe houses, and community reintegration, we provide a holistic response that restores dignity and builds safer communities.

Adolescent & Youth Sexual and Reproductive Health (AYSRH)

We champion young people’s right to make informed choices about their health and bodies. Our programs include sex education, contraceptive awareness, HIV/AIDS prevention, and maternal health. We also work with schools and communities to break stigma around reproductive health and promote safe, healthy futures for all youth.

Mental Health & Psychosocial Support

We create safe spaces for young people to talk, heal, and thrive. Through community outreach, peer support groups, and counseling, we address mental health challenges such as depression, anxiety, and drug abuse. Our goal is to build resilience and empower youth to lead healthy, balanced lives.

Disability Inclusion

We promote dignity, acceptance, and opportunity for people with disabilities, with a special focus on neurodiversity and children living with Cerebral Palsy. Through inclusive education, family support, awareness campaigns, and skills development, we ensure that everyone can participate fully and equally in society.

Youth and Adolescent Health Programme

At Jude’s CBO, we are committed to creating a future where young people can make informed choices about their health, education, and wellbeing. Our Youth and Adolescent Health Programme focuses on Sexual and Reproductive Health and Rights (SRHR) and mental health, with a strong emphasis on empowering adolescent girls and young women (AGYW).

  • Comprehensive sexuality education tailored for adolescents and youth.
  • Access to youth-friendly reproductive health services in partnership with local facilities.
  • Mentorship and peer-support networks to guide young people in making better life choices.
  • Mental health awareness and counseling support for resilience and wellbeing.

We are currently partnering with Tiko Africa (Triggerise) in Homa Bay County, working across 10 civic wards to provide contraceptives, HIV testing, and mental health support. Together, we are building a healthier, brighter future where no girl is left behind because of unplanned pregnancies or lack of support.

Community Cleanup Initiative

Our monthly cleanup initiative brings together residents, students, and local leaders to address waste and pollution challenges in Oyugis and Homa Bay County. By engaging the community, we are fostering environmental stewardship, reducing health risks, and creating cleaner spaces where children can safely play and families can live in dignity.

Breaking Barriers: Disability Inclusion

Jude’s CBO is committed to supporting neurodiverse individuals and persons with Cerebral Palsy through inclusive education, vocational training, and community awareness programs.

Our focus is on removing stigma, promoting dignity, and ensuring that every person—regardless of ability—can actively contribute to society and access equal opportunities. By breaking barriers, we are paving the way for a more inclusive and compassionate community.
